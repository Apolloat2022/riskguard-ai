# California Loan Restructuring Regulations

## CA-1. Rate Reduction Cap

For loans secured in California, the note rate may be reduced by up to 400 basis points
(4.00 percentage points) from the original contract rate — 100 basis points more generous
than the general baseline — reflecting California's consumer-protection emphasis on
affordability. Any reduction beyond 400 basis points still requires compliance-officer
sign-off under Section 6 of the general underwriting policy.

## CA-2. Forbearance Limits under California Civil Code

Forbearance periods for California borrowers may not exceed 15 months in any rolling
5-year period (extended from the 12-month general baseline). Deferred payments must be
added to the end of the loan term; capitalization of deferred interest into principal is
prohibited for California consumers regardless of the general policy's silence on the
point.

## CA-3. Required Disclosures (California)

In addition to the general disclosure requirements, California borrowers must receive a
Spanish-language translation of the remediation disclosure if the original loan
application was conducted in Spanish, and the 3-business-day rescission window required
by the general policy is extended to 5 business days for California consumers.

## CA-4. Escalation Requirements (California)

California cases must be escalated to compliance review whenever the proposed loan amount
exceeds $250,000, in addition to the general escalation triggers in Section 6 of the
underwriting policy.
