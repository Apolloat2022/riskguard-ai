# Underwriting & Loan Restructuring Policy — General

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes baseline underwriting standards and remediation procedures for
loans identified as high-risk by the automated risk-assessment model (default probability
greater than the configured trigger threshold). It applies to all consumer installment
loans serviced by RiskGuard AI, and is supplemented — never superseded — by
state-specific regulations. Where a state policy imposes a stricter requirement than this
general policy, the state policy controls.

## 2. General Restructuring Principles

Remediation plans must be individually tailored to the customer's demonstrated ability to
repay. A restructuring plan may combine one or more of: rate reduction, principal
forbearance, term extension, or a temporary interest-only period. Plans must not increase
the customer's total cost of credit beyond what is reasonably necessary to cure the
delinquency, and must not extend the loan term by more than 24 months beyond the original
maturity date without a documented hardship justification.

## 3. Baseline Rate Reduction Limits

Absent a stricter state limit, the note rate may be reduced by up to 300 basis points
(3.00 percentage points) from the original contract rate. Any reduction beyond 300 basis
points requires compliance-officer sign-off regardless of state.

## 4. Baseline Forbearance Limits

Absent a stricter state limit, a forbearance period may not exceed 12 months in any
rolling 5-year period, and payments deferred during forbearance must be added to the end
of the loan term rather than capitalized into the principal balance.

## 5. Disclosure Requirements

Every remediation plan must be disclosed to the customer in writing within 5 business days
of approval, and must clearly state: the new payment amount, the new maturity date, the
total finance charge under the new terms, and the customer's right to rescind within 3
business days of receipt.

## 6. Escalation and Compliance Review

A remediation plan must be escalated to a compliance officer (rather than auto-approved)
whenever: (a) the customer has been offered a restructuring in the preceding 12 months,
(b) the proposed rate reduction exceeds the baseline in Section 3, or (c) the customer's
default probability exceeds 0.90. Escalated cases route to `human_review` and require
explicit approval before the plan may be finalized.
