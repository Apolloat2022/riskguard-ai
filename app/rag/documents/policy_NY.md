# New York Loan Restructuring Regulations

## NY-1. Rate Reduction Cap

For loans secured in New York, the note rate may be reduced by up to 350 basis points
(3.50 percentage points) from the original contract rate, consistent with New York
Department of Financial Services guidance on affordable loan modifications.

## NY-2. Forbearance Limits (New York)

New York borrowers are subject to the general 12-month forbearance limit, but a second
forbearance period may be granted within the same rolling 5-year window if the customer
can document a qualifying hardship (job loss, medical emergency, or natural disaster)
distinct from the original hardship.

## NY-3. Required Disclosures (New York)

In addition to the general disclosure requirements, New York borrowers must be provided a
plain-language summary of the modification (no more than one page) alongside the full
disclosure, and the servicer must retain proof of delivery for no less than 6 years.

## NY-4. Escalation Requirements (New York)

New York cases must be escalated to compliance review whenever the customer has an open
complaint on file with the New York Department of Financial Services, in addition to the
general escalation triggers in Section 6 of the underwriting policy.
