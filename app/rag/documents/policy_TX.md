# Texas Loan Restructuring Regulations

## TX-1. Rate Reduction Cap

For loans secured in Texas, the note rate may be reduced by up to 300 basis points (3.00
percentage points) from the original contract rate — the same as the general baseline;
Texas imposes no additional rate-reduction generosity requirement.

## TX-2. Forbearance Limits (Texas)

Texas borrowers are subject to the general 12-month forbearance limit. Additionally, for
Texas home-equity-secured installment loans, forbearance offers must comply with Texas
Constitution Article XVI, Section 50 restrictions on modification of home-equity lending
terms; any modification implicating those restrictions must be escalated to compliance
review before an offer is made to the customer.

## TX-3. Required Disclosures (Texas)

In addition to the general disclosure requirements, Texas borrowers whose loan is secured
by a homestead must receive the disclosure by certified mail (not merely first-class
mail), and the servicer must obtain a signed acknowledgment of receipt.

## TX-4. Escalation Requirements (Texas)

Texas cases must be escalated to compliance review whenever the loan is secured by a
homestead, in addition to the general escalation triggers in Section 6 of the
underwriting policy.
